A DRHP exceeds 400 pages of legal jargon designed to protect the issuer, not inform the investor. AI transforms an unreadable document into an interrogatable intelligence asset — the same depth of scrutiny previously exclusive to institutional research desks, now accessible to everyone.

SEBI mandated Audio-Visual (AV) DRHP summaries in 2025 — the first regulatory requirement that a prospectus must be "interrogatable," not just "readable"

The Gap
A 400-page DRHP written in legal jargon creates a severe information asymmetry — institutions have analysts to parse it; retail investors rely on biased third-party commentary or skip it entirely.
AI Solution
A RAG-based pipeline transforms the DRHP into a dual-experience platform — a Risk-o-Meter for retail investors and a citation-anchored professional chatbot for analysts — both grounded in verifiable document sources.
Value Created
Every AI answer is anchored to a specific page number in the DRHP — eliminating the hallucination risk that makes GenAI dangerous in financial contexts.
Strategic Framing — The Verification Protocol
"For IPO intelligence, the AI's value is not in its answer but in its citation. By utilising a RAG architecture that anchors every response to a specific paragraph in the DRHP, the platform eliminates the 'black box' risk and provides retail investors with the same depth of scrutiny previously exclusive to institutional research desks."

Research Notes

⚠ Critical Risk — The Hallucination Problem
In Finance, 99% Correct is 100% Wrong
An LLM without grounding will occasionally hallucinate — generating a plausible-sounding but factually incorrect answer. In most contexts this is a nuisance. In IPO intelligence, it is a liability. A hallucinated debt figure, a fabricated risk disclosure, or a misattributed financial metric could directly influence a retail investor's decision. The RAG architecture is the solution: the model can only answer using text retrieved from the actual DRHP, and every answer must cite its source paragraph. If the document doesn't contain the answer, the system says so rather than inventing one. The Curation Layer in the Reference Vault enforces this — any response without a valid citation is rejected before reaching the user.
⚠ Data Challenge — Boilerplate vs Material Disclosure
Not Every Risk Is a Real Risk
A typical DRHP contains hundreds of pages of standard legal boilerplate — generic risk disclosures that every company must include but which reveal nothing specific about this issuer. Buried inside that boilerplate are the material disclosures that actually matter: a major supplier suing the company, a key customer representing 60% of revenue, a promoter pledging most of their shares. The AI must distinguish between a generic risk ("market volatility may affect our business") and a material one ("our single largest customer accounts for 58% of revenue and has not renewed their contract"). This requires a Sentiment Classifier trained specifically on financial legalese — general-purpose NLP models struggle to make this distinction reliably, which is why domain-specific fine-tuning is essential.
Technical Architecture
Vector DatabasePinecone or Milvus — stores document chunks as embedding vectors, enabling semantic search rather than keyword search across the full DRHP
Embedding ModelA financial domain-specific embedding model converts each paragraph into a vector that captures meaning, not just words — "debt repayment" and "liability reduction" are retrieved together
RAG PipelineUser query → embedding → similarity search → top-k relevant chunks retrieved → LLM generates answer grounded only in retrieved chunks → citation appended
Sentiment ClassifierFine-tuned transformer (FinBERT or equivalent) scores each paragraph as Cautious / Neutral / Confident — the Risk-o-Meter aggregates these scores across all Risk Factor sections
