Explainable AI (XAI) for Regulatory Reporting

High-performing AI models are opaque — they produce accurate decisions but cannot explain their reasoning in terms a regulator can evaluate. XAI layers transparency on top of existing AI without sacrificing performance.

SHAP / LIME LLM Narrative Generation Apache Kafka Partially Deployed Implementation Readiness: 7/10
The Gap
AI models make accurate trading and compliance decisions but cannot explain them — regulators require auditable reasoning, not just correct outcomes.
AI Solution
XAI frameworks generate mathematically verifiable, human-readable explanations layered on top of existing models without changing how they work.
Value Created
Powerful AI models can be used in regulated contexts that previously required simpler, less accurate rules — no performance trade-off.

Research Notes

Why This Matters Now
Regulatory driverSEBI, SEC, FCA, and ECB are all moving toward requiring firms to explain AI-driven decisions in trading and compliance contexts
MiFID II precedentAlready mandates explainability for certain algorithmic trading decisions across the EU
Current workaroundFirms use simpler, less accurate rules-based models just to satisfy audit requirements — XAI removes this trade-off entirely
Key Technical Components
SHAPSHapley Additive exPlanations — assigns each input feature a mathematically grounded contribution score to the model's output
LIMEAlternative to SHAP for cases requiring lighter computation; locally approximates the model around a specific prediction
Apache KafkaMessage queue that decouples the XAI pipeline from execution so trader latency is never impacted
LLM promptingAnchored to SHAP inputs — output is grounded in structured scores, not free generation, which prevents hallucination
Key Challenges & Mitigations
Faithfulness gapMitigated by storing raw SHAP scores alongside the narrative — regulators can mathematically verify any explanation at any time
Latency riskSolved by the async Kafka track — execution is never blocked regardless of XAI compute time
LLM hallucinationConstrained by strict prompt templates that anchor all output to structured SHAP inputs only
Feature mappingMapping 500 raw features to 15 business categories requires significant domain expertise at initial setup
