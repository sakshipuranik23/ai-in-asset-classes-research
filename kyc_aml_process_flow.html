KYC / AML Automation

Institutional client onboarding is buried in unstructured documents, manual entity checks, and periodic reviews that go stale the moment they are complete. AI transforms this from a one-time snapshot into a perpetual, living risk profile.

OCR / NLP Graph Analytics Agentic AI Partially Deployed Implementation Readiness: 7/10
The Gap
Client onboarding and ongoing counterparty monitoring are largely manual — consuming significant compliance capacity and producing data that is stale within days of completion.
AI Solution
A five-level AI maturity ladder progresses from document digitisation to fully autonomous onboarding, with perpetual monitoring replacing periodic reviews entirely.
Value Created
Onboarding time reduced from weeks to hours; AI monitors far more counterparties continuously than any human team ever could.

Research Notes

The Two Core Bottlenecks
Entity Resolution"Alphabet Inc." and "Google" may be the same entity — manually linking these across jurisdictions and languages is the single biggest time-sink in institutional KYC
Nested Ownership TrapLarge corporations have layers of subsidiaries; humans struggle to "peel the onion" to find the actual beneficial owner (UBO) at the top
Stale DataKYC is treated as a snapshot — by the time manual review is done, the entity may already have a new director or a new legal issue, making the data obsolete
Who Uses This Technology
Tier 1 BanksHighest volume and most complex corporate onboarding requirements — most advanced adopters of Levels 3–4
Brokers & Asset ManagersUse it to manage counterparty risk — ensuring a fund they trade with is not a front for sanctioned money
Exchanges & FintechsExchanges screen participants; fintechs (Revolut, Monex) use it to scale into new markets without hiring thousands of manual compliance officers
