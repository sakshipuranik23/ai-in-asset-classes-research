Cyclical vs Defensive Rotation via Macro AI Signals

Human advisors stay in cyclicals too long during a downturn — not because they lack data, but because of loss aversion and recency bias. The value here is not better data. It is a systematic, emotionless signal that forces the rotation the data already justifies.

Hidden Markov Models NLP / LLM Sentiment Nowcasting Random Forests Reinforcement Learning Partially Deployed
The Gap
Official macro data (GDP, CPI) is released weeks after the period ends — by the time it's published, markets have already moved. Human portfolio managers compound this with behavioural biases that delay necessary rotations.
AI Solution
A five-stage Macro-to-Micro pipeline: Nowcasting predicts official data before release, Hidden Markov Models identify the economic regime, and a Rotation Score drives automated portfolio tilts — systematically and without emotional delay.
Value Created
Rotating from cyclicals to defensives 2–4 weeks ahead of consensus converts a human lag into a systematic edge — capturing the inflection point before it becomes the prevailing market view.
Strategic Framing — Systematic Discipline
"Human advisors are prone to Loss Aversion and Recency Bias — staying in cyclicals too long during a downturn because the last few months felt good. An AI-driven macro signal provides an emotional firewall, enforcing a systematic rotation based on cold data rather than the prevailing market mood. The machine has no feelings about last quarter's returns."

Research Notes

⚠ Two Critical Failure Modes
The False Signal Trap & The Correlated Crash Problem
The False Signal Trap: Government macro data is frequently revised. A preliminary GDP reading of −0.2% (recession signal) that triggers a full defensive rotation may be revised three weeks later to +0.4% (expansion). The AI has executed a costly, disruptive rebalancing based on data that turned out to be wrong. The mitigation is a confirmation window — the signal must persist for N consecutive data points before triggering a rotation, and data revision buffers down-weight preliminary readings relative to final ones. Nowcast signals from alternative data provide a pre-check before official releases.

The Correlated Crash Problem: The cyclical/defensive distinction assumes a relatively orderly economic environment. In a true liquidity crisis — 2008, March 2020 — the correlation between all assets approaches 1. Defensives sell off alongside cyclicals as investors liquidate everything to raise cash. The AI's sector-rotation model has no meaningful edge in this regime because the model's fundamental assumption (that sectors behave differently) temporarily breaks down. The only mitigation is a crisis regime flag — when cross-asset correlations spike above a threshold, the system switches from sector-rotation mode to pure risk reduction (raising cash, buying volatility hedges) until correlations normalise.
Hidden Markov Models — Why This Algorithm
The core insightEconomic regimes (Expansion, Slowdown, Recession, Recovery) are not directly observable — they are hidden states inferred from observable macro indicators. HMMs are specifically designed for this structure.
UnsupervisedUnlike supervised ML that requires labelled historical regimes, HMMs discover the regime structure from the data itself — it finds the clusters that best explain the observed macro patterns without being told what to look for.
Transition probabilitiesThe model outputs not just the current regime but the probability of transitioning to each other regime — this is what populates the Regime Heatmap in the Strategist Dashboard.
LimitationHMMs assume regime transitions follow a fixed probability structure. Structural breaks — a pandemic, a war — can shift transition probabilities permanently, requiring model retraining.
