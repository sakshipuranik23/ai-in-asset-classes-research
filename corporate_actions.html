AI Corporate Actions Processing

Corporate Actions is the "final frontier" of post-trade automation. While trades execute in microseconds, a complex corporate action — a spin-off across multiple jurisdictions, a nested rights issue — can still take days of manual reconciliation. An error here is not a nuisance. It is a financial liability.

LLM / NLP Agentic AI Adversarial Reconciliation RPA + AI Financial Liability Risk Partially Deployed
The Gap
Two data vendors routinely report different Ex-Dates or ratios for the same event. A human must manually call the exchange to verify — a process that takes hours and fails at scale.
AI Solution
A three-tier agentic system: an Ops Dashboard surfacing only exceptions, a Reconciliation Brain that adversarially cross-references all vendor data, and an immutable Audit Ledger anchoring every decision.
Value Created
Near-elimination of "missing dividend" and "incorrect split" errors — events that currently cost the industry billions annually in claims, rework, and regulatory penalties.

Research Notes

⚠ AI Challenge — The Conditional Trap
When "Cash or Shares" Requires Client-Specific Reasoning
Many corporate actions are elective — the client can choose between receiving cash or additional shares, for example. Processing these automatically requires the AI to do something fundamentally more complex than data extraction: it must apply the client's standing instructions, tax profile, and portfolio context to make the optimal election. A client in a high tax bracket may prefer shares over a cash dividend that would trigger immediate tax liability. A client with a concentrated position may prefer cash to reduce exposure. This is not a lookup — it is client-specific reasoning, the kind of contextual decision-making that defines agentic AI. The system must integrate with the client's profile database and apply configurable decision rules, escalating to a human only when the standing instructions are ambiguous.
⚠ Data Challenge — The Vendor Conflict Problem
Two Trusted Sources, Two Different Answers
It is commonplace for Bloomberg and Reuters to report different Ex-Dates or ratios for the same corporate action event. Historically, a human operations analyst had to contact the exchange directly to adjudicate. At scale — thousands of events daily — this is impossible. The Adversarial Reconciliation approach resolves this by assigning credibility weights to each source: exchange feeds and DTCC are treated as authoritative; Bloomberg and Reuters as high-quality secondary. When conflicts arise, the system automatically adopts the authoritative source, flags the discrepancy in the audit trail, and routes a notification to the vendor for correction. Only unresolvable conflicts — where even the authoritative source is ambiguous — reach the human exceptions queue.
Technical Architecture
LLM / NLP ParsingExtracts action type, effective dates, ratios, tax implications, and elective options from unstructured announcement text — including non-English sources via multilingual models
Credibility WeightingConflict Resolution Agent assigns trust scores to each data source — exchange feed and DTCC score highest; Bloomberg and Reuters score high but below authoritative sources
Mathematical EngineTranslates corporate action terms into precise portfolio impacts — "1-for-10 split" becomes exact new share counts across every affected client position, including fractional share handling
RPA + AI PostingRobotic Process Automation executes the actual ledger update and exchange instruction — AI provides the verified data, RPA executes the mechanical update without human touch
