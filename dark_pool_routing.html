Research Notes

⚠ Critical Risk — The Ping Trap
Predatory HFTs Are Hunting You in the Dark
High-Frequency Trading firms deploy "pinging" strategies — sending tiny micro-orders into dark pools specifically to detect whether a large institutional order ("whale") is hiding there. Once they find you, they race to lit markets to front-run your remaining order, buying shares at the current price and selling them back to you milliseconds later at a worse price. This is the Ping Trap. The Scout Agent's micro-ping strategy is the direct countermeasure — it probes pools in ways that reveal liquidity without revealing order size or intent, flipping the information asymmetry back in the institution's favour.
⚠ Data Challenge — Adverse Selection
Was Your Fill Good Luck or a Trap?
When an order gets filled in a dark pool, there are two possible explanations: you found a genuine natural contra (good — you got a fair price), or a sophisticated algorithm "picked you off" — filled your order knowing the price was about to move against you. Distinguishing these two outcomes is the core data challenge. The signal is called a "Markout" — the price move in the 10–30 seconds after the fill. A consistently negative markout for a specific pool means that pool has high adverse selection. The Sentry Agent tracks this in real time and progressively reduces allocation to pools with deteriorating markout profiles — eventually killing the connection entirely if the signal is strong enough.
OODA Loop — Why This Model Works
ObserveScout monitors fill/fade data across all pools simultaneously — identifying which pools have real liquidity vs. ghost liquidity right now
OrientRouter scores each pool 0–100 for the specific order size and ticker — accounting for current volatility, time of day, and historical markout profile
DecideAgent decides to be Passive (wait for a natural match) or Aggressive (hit the midpoint) based on urgency, remaining order size, and time pressure
ActChild orders are fanned out to selected pools simultaneously; Sentry monitors each fill in real time and re-routes failed slices instantly without human intervention
